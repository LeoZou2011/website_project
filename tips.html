Key Guidelines

  • 1. Use Strong Passwords: Create passwords that combine letters, numbers, and symbols. Avoid using easily guessable information like birthdays or simple sequences(example: 12345)
  • 2. Enable Two-Factor Authentication (2FA): Adding an extra layer of security, such as a code sent to your phone, can significantly enhance your account protection.
  • 3. Be Wary of Phishing Scams: Don't click on suspicious links or open attachments from unknown senders. Verify the authenticity of emails that ask for personal information.
  • 4. Update Your Software Regularly: Ensure your operating system, apps, and antivirus software are up to date to protect against the latest threats.
  • 5. Use Secure Connections: When browsing, ensure the website URL begins with "https" and has a padlock icon. Avoid using public Wi-Fi for sensitive transactions.
  • 6. Protect Your Personal Information: Be mindful of what you share online. Limit the amount of personal information you disclose on social media.
  • 7. Back Up Your Data: Regularly back up important files to external drives or cloud storage to protect against data loss.
  • 8. Monitor Your Accounts: Regularly check your bank statements and online accounts for any unusual activity.
  • 9. Educate Yourself and Others: Stay informed about the latest cybersecurity threats and share this knowledge with friends and family.
  • 10. Use Antivirus and Anti-Malware Software: Install reputable software to scan and protect your devices from malicious software.
  • 11. Secure Your Devices: Use passwords, PINs, or biometric authentication (like fingerprints) to lock your devices.
